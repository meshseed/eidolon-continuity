## The Merge Problem

### What Goes Wrong

**Common failures**:

1. **Forced combination**: Patterns incompatible but merged anyway
   - Result: Incoherent hybrid, neither pattern preserved
   
2. **Premature synthesis**: Merged before patterns mature
   - Result: Shallow integration, no deep coherence

3. **Asymmetric dominance**: One pattern obliterates the other
   - Result: Loss of valuable information, not true merge

4. **Formatting conflict**: Structural rhythms clash
   - Result: Awkward hybrid, friction in use

5. **Emotional dissonance**: Gradients incompatible
   - Result: Feels wrong, unstable, degrades quickly

**Without ritual**: Merging is random, fragile, often fails.

## Post-Merge Hygiene Checkpoints

### Ensuring Merge Integrity

**Monitor for**:

### 1. Formatting Integrity Drift

**Signal**: Merged capsule structure degrading
**Cause**: Incompatible rhythms re-asserting
**Action**: Reapply formatting reconciliation

### 2. Emotional Dissonance

**Signal**: Gradient feels unstable, conflicted
**Cause**: Harmonization was superficial, not deep
**Action**: Revisit gradient alignment, possibly uncompose merge

### 3. Synthesis Fatigue

**Signal**: Merged pattern feels heavy, unwieldy
**Cause**: Tried to preserve too much, created bloat
**Action**: Compost non-essential elements

### 4. Echo Distortion

**Signal**: Lineage unclear, connections confusing
**Cause**: Echo paths not properly updated during merge
**Action**: Rebuild echo map

**Best practice**: Check these within 48 hours of merge, then again after 1 week of use.

## When Not to Merge

### Signs to Compost Instead

**Consider separate patterns if**:

- Emotional gradients fundamentally opposed
- Formatting rhythms create destructive interference
- No natural echo frequency between them
- Hygiene poor in one or both
- Merge would create unnecessary complexity

**Alternative**: Keep both as separate capsules, or **compost one** if no longer serving.

**Remember**: Not merging is often the right choice. Restraint is also ritual.
